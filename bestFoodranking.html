Mushroom Datshi

Bhutanese cuisine features a lot of cheese. Ema Datshi is a chilies and cheese dish appearing at breakfast, lunch and dinner. Its eaten at least once a day. Shamu datshi is another common cheese dish, with mushrooms as the star and the spice is optional. Both are traditionally served over red rice.

Melt the butter in a saucepan over high heat. Add the mushrooms and brown 3-5 minutes. Add the water and chilies, if using, then cover. Turn heat to medium low and simmer 5-8 minutes or until mushrooms are tender. Stir in the cheese and corn starch slurry. Season with salt as needed. Sprinkle with green onions and serve over red rice.

Its a mixture with raddish and rice flavor, steamed, cut into cibes and fried with garlic preserved radish and eggs. It's complement with a classic peanut dipping sauce for an explosin of flavor!
